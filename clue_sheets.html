Specialist's Field Guide

Method 1: Common Denominator (Safe)

1. Find a number both denominators can multiply into.

2. Multiply both the top and bottom of each fraction to get that new denominator.

3. Compare the new numerators. Higher numerator = Higher volume.

Method 2: Cross-Multiplication (Fast)

1. Multiply the Numerator of Tank A by the Denominator of Tank B. (Result A)

2. Multiply the Numerator of Tank B by the Denominator of Tank A. (Result B)

3. Compare Result A to Result B. The larger result indicates the larger tank.

BENCHMARK TIPS:

> If the numerator is exactly half the denominator, it's 1/2 FULL.

> If the numerator is less than half, the tank is LOW (< 1/2).

> If the numerator is more than half, the tank is HIGH (> 1/2).
